# Reader: small-domains

## Summary

The five smaller domain crates total ~6,300 LOC (koi-runtime 1,905; koi-dns 1,678; koi-health 1,070; koi-proxy 954; koi-udp 686) and all follow the same architectural template: a Core/Runtime facade, a tokio broadcast event channel, an axum `routes()` builder with a `paths` module, utoipa annotations plus a per-crate ApiDoc, an error enum mapped to shared ErrorCodes, and a Capability status impl. Structural discipline is genuinely high — domain crates never import each other, cross-domain reads go through koi-common::integration traits (MdnsSnapshot, CertmeshSnapshot, DnsProbe, ProxySnapshot, AliasFeedback), and the binary's orchestrator.rs is the single place where container lifecycle events become mDNS/DNS/health/proxy actions. But functional maturity varies wildly beneath the uniform surface: koi-dns is the most substantive (multi-source zone resolution with mDNS alias generation and certmesh SAN feedback), koi-udp is small but the only one with a real integration suite (787-line koi-embedded/tests/udp.rs), koi-health is a working sketch with serial check execution, koi-runtime is an alpha with half its advertised backends stubbed and no reconnect logic, and koi-proxy's data plane is outright broken: `listener.rs:46` registers route `"/*path"`, which panics under the workspace's resolved axum 0.8.8 (verified against axum source: "Path segments must not start with `*`... use `{*wildcard}`"), and the panic happens inside a `tokio::spawn` so it dies silently while `ProxyRuntime::status()` hardcodes `running: true`. A second latent panic sits in `watch_certs` (listener.rs:71-83), which calls `tokio::spawn` from notify's non-tokio watcher thread. No test anywhere starts a TLS listener, so the crate's core function — TLS termination — has plausibly never worked since the axum 0.8 upgrade.

The per-domain "tax" is large and mostly copy-paste. Inside a domain crate, the template costs ~250-300 lines before any domain logic: paths module + rel() (5 near-identical copies), routes() builder, error mapping (4 copies; koi-dns rolls its own local `error_response` at http.rs:322-332 instead of using `koi_common::http::error_response` like the other four), BROADCAST_CHANNEL_CAPACITY=256 redeclared per crate, a Capability impl (koi-runtime deviates with its own `capability_status()` method instead of the trait), and a start/stop Runtime wrapper — DnsRuntime (runtime.rs, 89 lines) and HealthRuntime (lib.rs:330-400) are line-for-line the same state machine. Three crates even carry ~50-60 lines each of identical unit tests that test tokio's broadcast channel rather than any domain logic. Outside the crate, the binary adds the real weight: commands/<domain>.rs (143-386 lines), cli.rs subcommand enums (~45-90 lines), surface.rs manifest entries (~160 lines for UDP's 5 commands), koi-client methods (~10 lines/endpoint), main.rs wiring duplicated a second time in platform/windows.rs (main.rs:540-630 vs windows.rs:485-580) and effectively a third time in koi-embedded's builder, plus dashboard/AppState/openapi/docs touches. For koi-udp — the smallest possible domain — total system cost is ~1,200 lines, of which novel domain logic (bind/relay/lease/reap) is ~300-350. Adding a new domain costs roughly 1,000-1,500 lines, of which 65-75% is scaffolding and at least half of that is mechanical copy-paste-with-renames.

On earning their place: koi-dns earns it most clearly — it is the bridge that makes mDNS discoveries, certmesh members, and static names resolvable to ordinary DNS clients and containers, and contains the most differentiated logic (aliases.rs, records.rs, zone.rs). koi-runtime plus the orchestrator is the project's thesis ("label a container, get announce/DNS/health/proxy for free") and earns its place as the philosophy demonstrator, but should shed its three "not yet implemented" backend stubs and two parsed-but-never-consumed metadata fields. koi-udp is well-made and cheap to keep, though its use case is niche. koi-health is half-redundant: its machine-health view is a re-projection of mDNS+certmesh data the daemon already has, and its service checks are commodity functionality — a strong merge-into-status/dashboard candidate. koi-proxy is the weakest case: it is broken at its core function, untested at the data plane, cannot handle WebSockets by construction (reqwest request/response forwarding, no upgrade support), and competes with Caddy/Traefik; its only unique value is certmesh cert integration. It should either be reduced to a ~200-line TCP/TLS passthrough (rustls + copy_bidirectional, which also fixes WebSockets) or shed.

## Strengths

- Consistent domain facade pattern across all five crates: opaque Core, broadcast events, domain-owned routes, thiserror enums mapped to shared ErrorCode — a new reader can learn one crate and navigate the other four
- Cross-domain decoupling via koi-common::integration traits (crates/koi-common/src/integration.rs) is clean and actually enforced — koi-dns consumes mDNS/certmesh through MdnsSnapshot/CertmeshSnapshot without importing those crates
- koi-dns has real, differentiated logic: three-source record snapshot (records.rs), mDNS-to-DNS alias generation with hostname-stable aliases and conflict-suffix avoidance (aliases.rs:71-87), certmesh SAN feedback loop (resolver.rs:709-739), private-IP guardrails and rate limiting (safety.rs)
- koi-udp is a model small domain: 686 LOC, single concern, excellent module docs (lib.rs:1-14), lease/reaper lifecycle, and the only one of the five with a substantial integration suite (crates/koi-embedded/tests/udp.rs, 787 lines)
- koi-runtime's normalized Instance/KoiMetadata model with label+env shorthand precedence (instance.rs:144-187) is well-designed and well unit-tested (10 tests covering precedence rules)
- The orchestrator (crates/koi/src/orchestrator.rs, 458 lines) is a genuinely good piece of architecture: single bridging point, resource tracking per instance, idempotent restart handling, cleanup on shutdown
- Error hygiene is mostly real: poisoned-lock recovery via unwrap_or_else(|e| e.into_inner()) (dns resolver.rs:450-470), no unwrap on serde in handlers, graceful fallbacks in SSE streams
- paths modules as single source of truth for axum routing AND the command manifest (e.g. koi-udp http.rs:22-35 referenced from surface.rs:1795) is a smart dedup of route strings

## Concerns

- BROKEN: koi-proxy data plane panics on startup under axum 0.8.8 — crates/koi-proxy/src/listener.rs:46 uses route("/*path") which axum 0.8 rejects with a panic (verified in axum-0.8.8/src/routing/path_router.rs:64: 'Path segments must not start with `*`... use `{*wildcard}`'); the panic occurs inside tokio::spawn (lib.rs:207-211) so it dies silently, and ProxyRuntime::status() (lib.rs:238-250) hardcodes running:true regardless
- Second latent panic in koi-proxy: watch_certs (listener.rs:71-83) calls tokio::spawn from notify's watcher callback, which runs on a non-tokio thread — first cert-change event panics the notify thread, silently killing hot cert reload
- Zero data-plane test coverage for proxy: the only integration test (koi-embedded/tests/embedded.rs:101-137) exercises config upsert/remove events, never a TLS listener — explaining why both panics went unnoticed
- koi-runtime reconnect story is half-finished: backend.rs:36-37 and :58-60 document that backends 'handle reconnection internally' and emit BackendDisconnected/BackendReconnected, but DockerBackend.watch() simply breaks on stream error (docker.rs:251-258) and start_watching never restarts it (lib.rs:165-171); BackendReconnected is never emitted anywhere; a Docker daemon restart permanently kills the runtime capability
- Three of six RuntimeBackendKind variants are stubs returning 'not yet implemented' (koi-runtime/lib.rs:252-260) yet are parseable from the CLI (backend.rs:87-97 accepts 'k8s', 'incus', 'systemd') — aspirational surface area
- Parsed-but-unconsumed metadata: KoiMetadata.health_kind (instance.rs:99-101) and KoiMetadata.certmesh (instance.rs:119-121) are extracted from labels but nothing in orchestrator.rs or anywhere in crates/koi consumes them — build_health_check (orchestrator.rs:419) ignores health_kind and decides HTTP-vs-TCP solely by health_path presence
- Dead code: koi-proxy config.rs:42-71 load_entries_with_certmesh has zero callers repo-wide; DnsCore.started_at (resolver.rs:118) is set and cloned but never read; DnsZone::fqdn_suffix (zone.rs:24) has no external callers
- koi-dns performance design: every DNS query stats the state file (StateCache.load, resolver.rs:446-462) and rebuilds the full snapshot including all mDNS alias derivation (resolve_local → snapshot() → build_snapshot, resolver.rs:250-253) — O(all mDNS records) work + a filesystem stat per query, uncached
- Copy-paste inside one function: the upstream-lookup-and-filter block appears twice verbatim in DnsHandler::handle_request (resolver.rs:622-648 and 649-669)
- koi-health checks execute serially in one loop (checker.rs:55-92): each check is awaited inline under a 1s ticker, so one slow target (up to timeout_secs=5) delays all others; no concurrency, no jitter; proxy_checks() regenerated every tick and bypass validate_check
- UdpRuntime::shutdown() cancels the daemon-wide token it was constructed with (lib.rs:268-270 cancels self.cancel, which main.rs:607 passes as a clone of the global cancel token — clones share state), so any future caller of udp.shutdown() outside daemon teardown would kill the whole daemon; commands/udp.rs:27-30 standalone bind contains a comment 'Keep the binding alive until the user terminates' immediately followed by cancel.cancel() — the code contradicts its comment
- ActiveBinding::touch() uses try_write (binding.rs:122-126) so a contended heartbeat silently fails to renew the lease while the HTTP handler returns success
- Inconsistencies across the template: koi-dns defines a private error_response (http.rs:322-332) instead of koi_common::http::error_response used by health/proxy/udp/runtime; koi-runtime implements its own capability_status() method (lib.rs:212-230) instead of the Capability trait the other four implement; koi-udp SSE defaults to infinite idle while mdns defaults to 5s
- Test coverage is heavily skewed: koi-dns/health/proxy have no tests/ directories; ~160 lines of near-identical unit tests in dns (resolver.rs:741-797), health (lib.rs:253-302), and proxy (lib.rs:262-323) test tokio's broadcast channel rather than any domain behavior
- Daemon wiring for these five domains is written twice — main.rs:540-630 and platform/windows.rs:485-580 — and koi-embedded's builder is a third, divergence-prone copy of the same construction logic
- koi-proxy forwarding cannot support WebSockets or any connection upgrade (forwarder.rs does reqwest request/response streaming only), a significant practical gap for a homelab reverse proxy fronting dashboards

## Maturity signals

- Beta-level structural hygiene: uniform facade pattern, workspace-pinned deps, thiserror everywhere, tracing instead of println, poisoned-lock recovery, no .unwrap() in production paths across all five crates
- Feasibility-level functional reality in koi-proxy: the crate's headline feature (TLS termination) panics at listener start under the pinned axum 0.8.8 and has never been integration-tested — the gap between structural polish and functional verification is the clearest maturity tell in the codebase
- Alpha-level koi-runtime: works for the Docker happy path with good unit tests (instance.rs has 10, heuristics 6, docker 4) and a tests/docker_integration.rs, but no reconnection, one of five event variants never emitted (Updated), and 3 of 6 backends stubbed
- Uneven test investment indicates recency ordering: koi-udp (newest pattern, 787-line integration suite) > koi-runtime (unit + docker integration) > koi-dns (unit tests on tricky logic) > koi-health/koi-proxy (only framework-testing unit tests)
- Phase markers in crate docs ('Phase 6', 'Phase 7', 'Phase 8' in dns/health/proxy lib.rs:1) reveal these were sequential feasibility sprints rather than independently hardened products
- Version string 0.2.202603241449 (date-stamped pre-1.0) and crates.io metadata recently added (commit 0d35d44) — publishing posture ahead of data-plane verification
- Recent commit history shows churn at the integration layer (bollard 0.18→0.20 upgrade, runtime orchestrator, announce shorthand, a reverted DnsConfig change) — active feature expansion, not consolidation

## Shed or simplify

- Shed or rewrite koi-proxy's data plane: replace the axum/reqwest HTTP-level forwarder (listener.rs + forwarder.rs, ~210 lines) with a rustls TLS-terminating TCP passthrough using tokio::io::copy_bidirectional — fixes the /*path panic, the notify/tokio panic, and the WebSocket gap simultaneously, in fewer lines; if not rewritten, at minimum fix route to /{*path}, capture a runtime Handle for the notify callback, make status() reflect actual listener liveness, and add one TLS integration test
- Merge koi-health's machine-health into the dashboard/status surface: collect_machine_health (machine.rs:66-133) is a pure projection of mDNS + certmesh + DNS data the daemon already holds — it needs no persistence, no CLI add/remove, and no domain crate; keep only the service-check half (service.rs + checker.rs, ~220 lines) or fold it into koi-common as a checker utility
- Extract a generic DomainRuntime<E> {start, stop, status} into koi-common: DnsRuntime (koi-dns/runtime.rs, 89 lines) and HealthRuntime (koi-health/lib.rs:330-400, ~70 lines) are the same state machine; saves ~150 lines now and standardizes every future domain
- Delete dead code: load_entries_with_certmesh (koi-proxy/config.rs:42-71), DnsCore.started_at (resolver.rs:118), DnsZone::fqdn_suffix accessor (zone.rs:24-26), and koi-dns's private error_response in favor of koi_common::http::error_response (~80 lines plus an eliminated inconsistency)
- Delete the three copies of broadcast-channel self-tests (dns resolver.rs:741-797, health lib.rs:253-302, proxy lib.rs:262-323, ~160 lines) — they test tokio, not Koi; replace with one real behavioral test each (e.g. add_entry emits EntryUpdated through the core, which none currently verify end-to-end)
- Remove Systemd/Incus/Kubernetes from RuntimeBackendKind and from_str_loose until at least one is implemented (koi-runtime/lib.rs:252-260, backend.rs:87-97) — an enum of two real variants is honest; six with three stubs is marketing
- Remove or wire the unconsumed koi.health.kind and koi.certmesh labels (instance.rs:99-101, 119-121) — currently they parse, appear in OpenAPI schema, and do nothing
- Consolidate the triple daemon-wiring: make main.rs and platform/windows.rs both build cores through koi-embedded's Builder (which already exists and is tested) instead of maintaining three hand-rolled copies of DnsRuntime/ProxyRuntime/HealthRuntime/UdpRuntime/RuntimeCore construction
- Standardize the routes/paths/ApiDoc/error-mapping template as a koi-common macro or at least one canonical reference implementation: the paths-module + rel() + routes() + map_error block is 5× ~50 lines of copy-paste that silently drifts (dns already diverged on error_response, runtime on Capability)
- Reconsider koi-udp's token topology: give UdpRuntime its own child token (parent.child_token()) so shutdown() cannot cancel the daemon-wide tree (lib.rs:111-126, 268-270), and either implement or delete the contradictory standalone bind path (commands/udp.rs:7-49)
